Build Your Starter

What Is a Sourdough Starter?

A starter is a mixture of flour and water that captures wild yeast and bacteria from your environment. Over several days of regular feeding, it becomes alive — bubbly, active, and ready to leaven bread without any commercial yeast.

What You'll Need

  • 🌾Whole wheat or rye flour — for the first few days (more wild yeast on the bran). Switch to bread flour after day 3 if you prefer.
  • 💧Filtered or room-temp water — chlorinated tap water can slow fermentation. Let it sit 30 min or use filtered.
  • 🫙A clean glass jar — at least 500 ml so it has room to rise.
  • 📏A kitchen scale — grams are far more accurate than cups for this.

Day-by-Day Guide

Day 1

Mix it up

Combine 50 g whole wheat flour + 50 g water (room temp) in your jar. Stir until no dry flour remains. Cover loosely (not airtight) and leave at room temperature — ideally 21–24 °C / 70–75 °F.

Don't worry if nothing happens today. It's normal.
Day 2

First signs of life

You may see small bubbles or a slightly sour smell. Discard all but 50 g of your starter, then feed it: add 50 g flour + 50 g water. Stir well and cover loosely.

The discard keeps the quantity manageable and prevents acidity buildup.
Day 3

Getting active

Repeat the discard and feed from Day 2. By now you should see more bubbles and a tangy, slightly alcoholic smell — that's fermentation working. Start marking the jar with a rubber band to track how much it rises.

Days 4–6

Feed twice a day

Discard down to 50 g, then feed 50 g flour + 50 g water every 12 hours (morning and evening). Your starter should start doubling in size between feedings and smell pleasantly sour and yeasty.

If it smells like nail polish remover (acetone), it's hungry — feed it more often.
Day 7

The float test

Drop a small spoonful of starter into a glass of water. If it floats, it's ready to bake with — full of trapped gas from active fermentation. If it sinks, give it another day or two of twice-daily feedings.

✅ Floats = ready to bake. ❌ Sinks = keep feeding.

Ongoing Maintenance

🍞
Baking Weekly
Keep on the counter. Feed once or twice a day, always discard first.
❄️
Baking Rarely
Store in the fridge. Feed once a week. Take out 1–2 days before baking to wake it up.
⚖️
Feed Ratio
1:1:1 is standard — equal parts starter, flour, water by weight.
🌡️
Temperature
Warmer = faster rise. Cooler = slower, more sour. Aim for 21–24 °C.
